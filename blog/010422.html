Quantum Simulation in Smart Contracts: Part One

January 4, 2022

Banks are using quantum computing to build financial instruments that are hundreds of times more efficient than their counterparts in the traditional computing space. While some public quantum computing initiatives exist, a vast majority of the organization's own their computing machines despite the high cost of ownership and operation.

DeFi (Decentralized Finance) smart contracts are designed to replicate the behavior of prior financial products without the same issues of institutional ownership. Currently there are no smart contract enabled quantum computing blockchain networks, but it is possible to prepare for the future by simulating a quantum computer in existing smart contracts by utilizing oracles. Next week I will delve deeper into the possibilities of a quantum oracle.
